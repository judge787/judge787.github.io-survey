Ph.D. Students

Hang Yin

Co-supervised with Scott Krayenhoff, Hang's research involves integration of Building Energy Models (BEMs) into the Global Environmental Multiscale (GEM) model. The GEM model is the primary weather forecast and climate model used by the Government of Canada. Hang will additionally consider integrating sustainable building components such as green and photovoltaic roofs into the GEM model.
